Best Wedding Shoes, From Heels to Sneakers

Publish date: 2024-09-09

Planning a wedding can get the best of even the most organized and level headed people: There are so many details you wouldn’t even realize you had to think about. For one, your wedding shoes. While many wedding gown designs feature a skirt that goes to the floor, which means you could perhaps get away with wearing flip-flops as many brides have decided to do, you could end up throwing off the proportion of your dress (and tripping over your hem). Luckily, there are options for wedding shoes that are stylish yet comfortable enough for your important day.

What to consider when buying wedding shoes

Wedding shoes have a lot of expectations to fulfill: They need to look sophisticated and sexy while also being practical—you want to be able to enjoy your day without screaming in pain. There are so many options available that you may not know where to start, but asking yourself these questions can help streamline your choices.

What’s your budget?

How much money you have set aside for your wedding shoes will give you an idea on where to start looking brand-wise. Designers that specialize in the wedding market tend to price higher than non-wedding shoes (for instance, Bella Belle offers a mid-range price point of $300-$400). If you have your heart set on a high-end designer shoe, expect to pay $600-1000.

Where will the wedding take place?

An outdoor wedding that takes place on grass may not be the best place to wear stilettos—while an indoor, formal setting may call for a closed-toe shoe.

Will there be dancing?

If there’s going to be dancing and you love to dance, make sure that whatever shoe you choose, you can easily kick up your heels while wearing them.

Where should you buy wedding shoes?

You can buy wedding shoes pretty much anywhere. Most brands have at least a few styles that can work for a wedding.

What are some popular trends in wedding shoes?

One of the biggest trends in wedding shoes is comfort; things like block heels, short boots, flats, and even sneakers. Don’t be afraid to express your own individuality. If you’re not a person who ever wears stiletto heels and you don’t want to start doing so on your wedding day, there is nothing wrong with that. It’s your day, after all.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

Is it OK to wear flat shoes to a wedding?

It’s absolutely okay to wear flat shoes to a wedding. Many people have gotten married in flat shoes and many people attend weddings wearing flat shoes. We believe fashion is a personal choice and you should wear what makes you the most comfortable (within reason, of course: If you’re a guest at a wedding with a dress code, please honor that dress code, though you can wear flats with your formal frock if you want).

Do wedding shoes have to be white?

Wedding shoes do not have to be white. Many brides wear a blue shoe to satisfy the “something blue” and others just like to add a splash of color to the otherwise neutral pallet.

How much should I expect to spend on wedding shoes?

You can expect to pay anywhere from $39 to $2,000 or more for wedding shoes. Budget shoes may not last much longer than the wedding itself, but there’s no shame in choosing to purchase more wallet-friendly footwear. If you do decide to splurge, you’ll likely be able to wear those shoes for years to come.

How can I ensure my wedding shoes fit perfectly?

To ensure your wedding shoes fit perfectly, study the designer’s sizing chart first instead of ordering the “size you always wear”—especially if you’re going for sky-high heels. Then, preparation is everything. Many people do a test run by wearing them at home for a day to make sure they won’t end up in excruciating pain after an hour or two. (On a related-to-wedding-prep note, we also encourage you to give your shapewear a test run as well for the same reasons.)
